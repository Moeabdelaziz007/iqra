# Dhul-Qarnayn (ذو القرنين)

## Quranic References
- **Surah Al-Kahf (18:83-98):** The primary narrative of Dhul-Qarnayn's journeys to the East, West, and the construction of the barrier.
- **Surah Al-Kahf (18:83):** "And they ask you, [O Muhammad], about Dhul-Qarnayn. Say, \"I will recite to you about him a report.\""

## Tafsir Summary (Ibn Kathir)
Dhul-Qarnayn was a righteous king and a powerful ruler whom Allah gave the means to travel across the earth. He reached the setting place of the sun and its rising place. His most famous achievement was building a massive barrier of iron and copper to protect a vulnerable people from the corruption of Gog and Magog (Ya'juj and Ma'juj). Ibn Kathir notes that he was not Alexander the Great, as Alexander was a polytheist, whereas Dhul-Qarnayn was a monotheist.

## Archaeological/Historical Evidence
- **The Great Wall of Gorgan:** Some researchers suggest this wall in modern-day Iran could be related to the barrier, though it doesn't perfectly match the Quranic description of iron and copper.
- **Cyrus the Great:** Many modern scholars, including Abul Kalam Azad, identify Dhul-Qarnayn with Cyrus the Great of Persia, who was known for his justice and monotheistic leanings.

## Open Mysteries
- The exact location of the barrier between the two mountains.
- The identity of Gog and Magog in a modern geopolitical or eschatological context.

## Confidence Levels
- **Quranic Narrative:** 100% (Divine Revelation)
- **Identification as Cyrus:** 60% (Strong historical parallels but not definitive)
- **Location of Barrier:** 15% (Highly debated and potentially hidden)

⚠️ *Note: The barrier remains one of the greatest physical mysteries of the Quran.*
