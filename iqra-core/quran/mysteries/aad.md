# The People of Aad (قوم عاد)

## Quranic References
- **Surah Al-A'raf (7:65):** "And to the 'Aad [We sent] their brother Hud..." (وَإِلَىٰ عَادٍ أَخَاهُمْ هُودًا)
- **Surah Hud (11:50):** "And to 'Aad [We sent] their brother Hud..." (وَإِلَىٰ عَادٍ أَخَاهُمْ هُودًا)
- **Surah Ash-Shu'ara (26:123-140):** Detailed account of Hud's message and Aad's rejection.
- **Surah Al-Fajr (89:6-8):** "Have you not seen how your Lord dealt with 'Aad - [with] Iram, who had lofty pillars, the like of which were not produced in [all] the land?"

## Tafsir Summary (Ibn Kathir)
Ibn Kathir notes that the people of Aad were the successors of the people of Noah. They were physically powerful and lived in the region of Al-Ahqaf (sand dunes) in southern Arabia. They built lofty structures (Iram) to show off their strength and wealth. Despite Hud's warnings against their idolatry and arrogance, they persisted until they were destroyed by a "barren wind" (ريح عقيم) that lasted seven nights and eight days.

## Archaeological/Historical Evidence
- **Ubar (Atlantis of the Sands):** In the early 1990s, satellite imagery helped identify the remains of a city in Oman that many archaeologists believe is the lost city of Ubar, potentially linked to the Quranic Iram.
- **Location:** The region of Al-Ahqaf corresponds to the modern-day Rub' al Khali (Empty Quarter).

## Open Mysteries
- The exact location of Iram of the Pillars remains a subject of debate.
- The specific nature of the "lofty pillars" and whether they refer to physical architecture or the stature of the people.

## Confidence Levels
- **Quranic Narrative:** 100% (Divine Revelation)
- **Geographic Location (Al-Ahqaf):** 95% (Strong historical/linguistic consensus)
- **Identification of Ubar as Iram:** 70% (Significant archaeological support but not definitive)

⚠️ *Note: Archaeological findings are subject to ongoing research.*
