# The Prophet's Decision-Making (قرارات الرسول)

## Hadith References
- **Sahih al-Bukhari 100 (Knowledge):** The importance of qualified leadership and the danger of ignorant leaders.
- **Sahih al-Bukhari 6496 (Trust):** The role of Amanah (Trust) as the foundation of the heart and decision-making.
- **Sahih Muslim 1827 (Justice):** The reward for those who are equitable in their judgment and authority.
- **Sahih al-Bukhari 7376 (Mercy):** Mercy as a guiding principle in all interactions.

## Character Analysis
The Prophet's (ﷺ) decisions were characterized by a perfect balance of these four core values. He sought knowledge (Shura/Consultation), maintained absolute trust (Amanah), applied unwavering justice (Adl), and was guided by profound mercy (Rahmah). His decisions were not just tactical but were rooted in a "Sovereign Integrity" that aligned with divine will.

## Decision Patterns
- **Consultation (Shura):** Actively seeking input from companions despite receiving revelation.
- **Prioritizing Mercy:** Choosing the easier of two options unless it was a sin.
- **Absolute Justice:** Applying the law equally, even to his own family.
